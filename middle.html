Part II

The Underground Miro Market

A secret station beneath the city where maps redraw themselves, memories are bottled, and every traveler must prove why they came.

Middle

The market tests the travelers

The impossible tram did not descend so much as persuade the city to rearrange itself downward. It slipped between buildings, through a wall of mist, under a bridge that should have been demolished, and into a tunnel lined with blue tiles and old advertisements. The windows showed vanished streets and passengers who were not there. The living ticket rested in Rin’s hand, warm and watchful.

The tram stopped in a vast underground station transformed into The Underground Miro Market. Iron arches held up a ceiling lost in shadow. Neon signs glowed over damp platforms. Strange sellers offered spare shadows, bottled echoes, self-changing maps, old tram bells, brass handles, route signs, cracked lanterns, and vending-machine courage with questionable expiry dates.

At the entrance stood Tanti Zora, the guardian of the secret routes. She looked like an elderly ticket inspector, short and sharp-eyed, with a navy-blue uniform, a red scarf, a huge ring of keys, and an old ticket puncher. She inspected the living ticket and refused to let the travelers pass until they told the truth about why they had come.

Mara admitted she was not only chasing an article; she was angry that the city was being erased while everyone acted as if memory did not matter. Dodo admitted he was terrified and wanted better lighting, but he would not abandon his friends. Rin admitted that she needed to know whether her grandfather had been a liar, a guardian, or someone who had carried a burden alone. The ticket accepted them and stamped itself: “Access Granted — Miro Market.”

Inside the market, Rin found the second part of her grandfather’s trail: a brass tram bell engraved with his initials. When she rang it, no ordinary sound emerged. Instead, invisible signs appeared across the walls, revealing hidden routes beneath the city and a lantern-shaped mark at their center. Rin realized that her grandfather had helped maintain the hidden tram network and protect the city’s forgotten memories.

Victor returned, no longer content to wait outside the rules. He had forced his way into the market using stolen tram technology. He released bottled echoes to create chaos, filling the station with old announcements, laughter, arguments, and fragments of lost lives. While sellers ran and shadows crawled loose from their hooks, Victor tried to seize the living ticket and the bell.

The heroes escaped through the market using their different strengths. Mara followed historical route clues hidden on old posters. Rin used the bell to reveal invisible signs. Dodo repaired a broken signal box with a charger cable, a coin, and the wrapper from an “Universal Emergency Biscuit.” Tanti Zora held back Victor’s people and warned them that the collector had stolen enough tram parts to build an illegal access machine.

The market is the story’s labyrinth: funny, dangerous, and full of traded memories.

Plot passage: After the escape, the ticket stamps its final destination: “City Hall Roof — Lantern Archive Access Pending.” The market has revealed the path, but Victor has already begun forcing his own shortcut.
